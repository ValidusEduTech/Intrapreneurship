What is Intrapreneurship?

Creating Value from Within

Intrapreneurship is the practice of applying entrepreneurial skills and mindsets within an existing organization rather than starting a new venture.

"Intrapreneurs are dreamers who do. Those who take hands-on responsibility for creating innovation of any kind within an organization."
‚Äî Gifford Pinchot III, who coined the term in 1978

Intrapreneurship

Working within an existing structure

Leverages organizational resources

Lower personal financial risk

Navigates existing systems

Implementation within established brand

Entrepreneurship

Building something from scratch

Secures own resources and funding

Higher personal financial risk

Creates new systems

Building a new brand identity

Core Intrapreneurial Competencies

Skills That Drive Innovation from Within

1. Initiative

Proactively identifying opportunities rather than waiting for assignments

2. Value Creation

Developing solutions that benefit the organization or its stakeholders

3. Adaptability

Navigating organizational structures and adapting to constraints

4. Innovation Thinking

Finding creative approaches to existing challenges

5. Influence Without Authority

Gaining buy-in without formal power

Why Is Intrapreneurship Relevant Today?

Preparing for Future Career Success

Future of Work: Organizations increasingly value employees who can innovate

Career Mobility: Intrapreneurial skills transfer across industries and roles

National Education Policy (NEP): Aligns with emphasis on practical skills and innovation

Global Citizenship: Prepares you to create value in diverse organizational contexts

Resilience: Builds capacity to thrive amidst rapid workplace change

Intrapreneurship Across Career Pathways

Applications in Various Fields

Higher Education

Developing innovative research projects, creating student initiatives, or improving campus systems

Employment

Proposing process improvements, developing new products/services, or creating internal programs

Entrepreneurship

Testing ideas within established frameworks before launching independently

Family Business

Modernizing operations or expanding into new markets within the existing business

Real-World Example

Intrapreneurship in Action

Post-it Notes were created when 3M scientist Spencer Silver developed an adhesive that didn't stick permanently. Rather than discarding this "failure," his colleague Art Fry saw an opportunity to create repositionable bookmarks. They advocated within 3M to develop what became one of the company's most successful products.

Key Questions to Reflect On

Prepare for the Event

What existing systems do you interact with daily (school, clubs, community)?

What improvements or innovations could make these systems more effective?

What barriers might prevent you from implementing these changes?

What resources within these systems could you leverage to create value?
