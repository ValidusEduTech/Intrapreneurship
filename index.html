INTRAPRENEURSHIP

Your Guide to Creating Value from Within

✧WHAT IS INTRAPRENEURSHIP?

Intrapreneurship is the practice of applying entrepreneurial skills and mindsets within an existing organization rather than starting a new venture.

"Intrapreneurs are dreamers who do. Those who take hands-on responsibility for creating innovation of any kind within an organization." — Gifford Pinchot III, who coined the term in 1978

✧INTRAPRENEURSHIP vs. ENTREPRENEURSHIP

Intrapreneurship Entrepreneurship
Working within an existing structure Building something from scratch
Leverages organizational resources Secures own resources and funding
Lower personal financial risk Higher personal financial risk
Navigates existing systems Creates new systems
Implementation within established brand Building a new brand identity

✧CORE INTRAPRENEURIAL COMPETENCIES

  • Initiative: Proactively identifying opportunities rather than waiting for assignments
  • Value Creation: Developing solutions that benefit the organization or its stakeholders
  • Adaptability: Navigating organizational structures and adapting to constraints
  • Innovation Thinking: Finding creative approaches to existing challenges
  • Influence Without Authority: Gaining buy-in without formal power

✧WHY IS INTRAPRENEURSHIP RELEVANT TODAY?

  • Future of Work: Organizations increasingly value employees who can innovate
  • Career Mobility: Intrapreneurial skills transfer across industries and roles
  • National Education Policy (NEP): Aligns with emphasis on practical skills and innovation
  • Global Citizenship: Prepares you to create value in diverse organizational contexts
  • Resilience: Builds capacity to thrive amidst rapid workplace change

✧INTRAPRENEURSHIP ACROSS CAREER PATHWAYS

Higher Education: Developing innovative research projects, creating student initiatives, or improving campus systems

Employment: Proposing process improvements, developing new products/services, or creating internal programs

Entrepreneurship: Testing ideas within established frameworks before launching independently

Family Business: Modernizing operations or expanding into new markets within the existing business

✧REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE

Post-it Notes were created when 3M scientist Spencer Silver developed an adhesive that didn't stick permanently. Rather than discarding this "failure," his colleague Art Fry saw an opportunity to create repositionable bookmarks. They advocated within 3M to develop what became one of the company's most successful products.

✧KEY QUESTIONS TO REFLECT ON BEFORE THE EVENT

  1. What existing systems do you interact with daily (school, clubs, community)?
  2. What improvements or innovations could make these systems more effective?
  3. What barriers might prevent you from implementing these changes?
  4. What resources within these systems could you leverage to create value?
